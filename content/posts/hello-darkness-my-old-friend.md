Five words, and the world holds its breath.

Hello darkness, my old friend. Not a cry. Not a plea. A greeting. The kind
you offer someone who has been standing at your door so long they no longer
need to knock. There is no surprise in the speaker's voice, no protest. Only
the quiet recognition of something that was always going to return. The way you
recognize your own reflection in a window you did not mean to look into.

And that is what stops you. Not the melody, not the guitar, not Garfunkel's
voice climbing like smoke through a cathedral. It is the calm. The terrible,
beautiful calm of a person who has learned to set a place at the table for
their own loneliness. There is a comfort in that stillness. A velvet gravity.
The same pull you feel driving alone at night with the city smeared in neon,
behind rain-streaked glass, Phil Collins building somewhere beneath the hum of
the engine, and for reasons you cannot name, you do not want to go home yet.

I've come to talk with you again. Of course it has. Darkness does not forget
your address. It knows the hours you keep, the ones after midnight when the
thoughts you outran all day finally catch up. And the speaker does not flinch.
He opens the door. He lets it in. There is something almost sacred in that
surrender, something most of us spend entire lifetimes refusing to do.
Something that looks, from the outside, like giving up. But from the inside
feels like the first honest breath in weeks.

Then the vision, softly creeping, leaving its seeds while you were sleeping.
This shifts from confession into something closer to prophecy. The seeds were
planted in unconscious hours, in the unguarded dark behind closed eyes.
Whatever truth the speaker carries, he did not choose it. It chose him. It grew
while he was not looking, and now it lives inside him, restless and luminous,
within the sound of silence.

Within the sound of silence. Four words that should contradict each other and
instead describe a place every human being has visited. The sound of silence is
the hum beneath all noise. It is the frequency your mind tunes to at 3 a.m.
when every distraction has been stripped away and you are left with nothing but
yourself. It is not empty. It has texture, weight, temperature. Sometimes it
even has a colour. Indigo, maybe. The bruised purple of a sky that cannot
decide if it is night or morning.

In restless dreams I walked alone, narrow streets of cobblestone. The landscape
is half-real, half-imagined, a city built from insomnia and memory. The neon
light that stabs the night, the cold and damp, the ten thousand people talking
without speaking, hearing without listening. It is a world overfull of people
and utterly vacant of connection.  Everyone is present. No one is there. And
there is a strange romance in moving through it untouched. The lone figure
under streetlights. The silhouette against wet pavement. Depression has always
known how to dress well in the dark.

This is the ache that lives inside: not solitude itself, but solitude in the
middle of a crowd. The particular loneliness of being surrounded by voices that
never quite reach you, of writing songs that no one will sing. The speaker is
not alone on a mountaintop. He is alone in a subway car, in a crowded room, and
at a dinner party where every conversation skates along the surface of things
and never once breaks through. And part of him, the part he does not say aloud,
has stopped wanting it to.

People are writing songs that voices never share. There is a whole quiet
tragedy folded into that image. Not the absence of creation, but the absence of
reception. The songs exist. The stories are written. But the voices do not
carry them. Something in the mechanics of human closeness has broken, and no
one has noticed because noticing would require the very silence everyone is
working so hard to avoid.

Truth has not disappeared. It has simply migrated to the places no one looks.
It lives in graffiti on the streets, in the dank place under a bridge, and in
the margins of books and songs, the spaces polite society steps over. The
silence is not a void. It is a vessel. It holds everything we are too busy or
too frightened to say aloud.

The story does not resolve. It does not offer comfort or a path forward. It
simply names what is, and then it stops, and the silence it described rushes in
to fill the space where the music was. That is its final gift. It does not
describe silence. It creates it. It leaves you sitting in the very thing it was
talking about, the engine idling, the city lights blurred and beautiful and
impossibly far away, and you realize the darkness was never the enemy. It was
the only one that stayed.

Five words. A hand extended into the dark. And the dark, quiet and ancient and
infinitely patient, reaching back.
